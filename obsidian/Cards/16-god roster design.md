Placeholder for the follow-up plan that drafts the 16 gods, their powers, and their level-1 blessings.

## Why deferred
The combat brainstorm (2026-05-14) settled the framework: each god grants one **power** + one **multiplicatively-stacking blessing** that modifies that power. The roster itself is content design, not architecture, and warrants its own focused brainstorm.

## What the follow-up plan needs to settle
- Which 16 gods, drawn from the biblical Mediterranean / Levantine pantheon (Asherah, Ishtar, Hathor, Marduk, Baal Hadad, Yahweh-as-warrior, etc.). Aim for cultural variety — Canaanite, Babylonian, Assyrian, Egyptian, Hebrew.
- For each god: their power (3 cost tiers: light / medium / heavy — see [[Power system]]).
- For each god: their level-1 blessing (a clean multiplicative modifier on the power).
- Cultural / biome affinity: which god aligns with which cultural ruin biome (see [[Cultural ruin biomes]]).

## Canonical examples (from brainstorm)
- Baal Hadad: power = Chain Lightning (medium tier, 5 base bounces). Blessing = +20% bounces.
- Hathor: power = Heal (medium tier). Blessing = +X% heal magnitude (or +1 turn duration if HoT).
- Ishtar: power = Burst damage / love-and-war themed (medium-heavy). Blessing = +X% damage.

These three are sketches for design-validation; not committed.

## Constraints
- Each tier (light/medium/heavy) should have ~5 powers so the run-pick from your deck of 4 has variety.
- Powers within a tier should cover targeting variety: single-target, AOE, line, self-buff, summon.
- Blessings should always be a clean multiplicative modifier (not flat additive) — see [[Blessing system]].

Source: design plan dated 2026-05-14 (16-god roster explicitly out of scope of that plan).
